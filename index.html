Anaplastic lymphoma kinase (ALK)

Abnormalities in the ALK pathway result in cancer development, such as anaplastic large cell lymphoma, neuroblastoma, or non-small lung cancer. Carcinogenesis results from ALK gene fusion with oncogenes, gene mutation, amplification, and an increase in protein expression. Usually, these genetic changes lead to constitutively active ALK receptor tyrosine kinase, consequently activating downstream signaling pathways and leading to uncontrolled cell growth, anti-apoptotic signaling, and cellular transformation. Della Corte C.M., Viscardi G., Di Liello R., Fasano M., Martinelli E., Troiani T., Ciardiello F., Morgillo F. 2018. Role and targeting of anaplastic lymphoma kinase in cancer. Mol. Cancer, 17.
