G4, G5 loops and the HVR

G4 loop:

  • N1 and N2 atoms of guanine donate H-bonds to D119. Accordingly, this interaction provides high specificity for guanine1.
  • K117 donates H-bonds to the main chain carbonyl of G13 in the P-loop, and to a water molecule, through which N85 is connected. N85A mutation has a severe impact on hydrolysis and nucleotide binding1,3.
  • The side chain amide of N116 donates an H-bond to the main chain carbonyl group of V14, and thus N116 together with K117 of G4 provide stabilisation to the P-loop1.

G5 loop:

  • The hydroxyl group of S145 donates an H-bond to the side chain of D119, helping position it for interaction with guanine1.
  • Side chain of T144 donates an H-bond to N116, helping position it for interaction with V14
  • Strong H-bond interaction between A146 and O6 of guanine results in high specificity for guanine1.
  • K147 forms salt bridge interactions with D30 and E31 of Switch I2.
  • T148 plays an important role in stabilising the base recognition loop of the G5 motif by forming intra-loop H-bonds3.

Hypervariable region (HVR):

  • Hypervariable region consists of amino acids from 166-189.
  • Flexible linker domain with two consecutive proline residues (P173 and P174) is critical in H-Ras's activation and its invasive potential in human breast epithelial cells4.
HVR contains two signal sequences that target H-Ras to the plasma membrane:
  • CAAX box at the extreme C-terminus. The CAAX box is sequentially post-translationally modified to render it more hydrophobic: the cysteine is farnesylated, the AAX sequence is removed by proteolysis, and the remaining cysteine is carboxymethylated5.
  • The second signal sequence consists of palmitoylation of cysteines 181 and 1845.
